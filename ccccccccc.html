Poonchi Wool

❮ ❯

Jammu and Kashmir (UT) is the second-largest producer of wool in India. JK UT produces approximately 70 lakh kilogrammes of wool per year.Globally renowned Pashmina is considered the finest craftsmanship in the world, which transforms the exceptionally warm and delicate wool threads into fabulous shawls. In India, the fleece of the most exotic goat, known as Pashm, which lives at high altitudes in the Himalayas, is exceptional wool. The quality and warmth of Pashmina are rare and revered all over the world. Pashmina has fascinated kings, royals, and people all over the world with its magical allure and traditional grace.

Poonchi wool is one of the best wools in Jammu and Kashmir. It is soft, shining, and very warm. Poonchi sheep is locally known as Pahari sheep and is reared by the nomadic communities of Jammu Province, namely the Baherwals, Gadhi, and Pahari. These sheep are maintained by farmers without any scientific management or breeding practices. The economic focus of these sheep is mutton and wool production. Poonchi sheep are mostly white, black, and brown in colour. The research study of poonchi wool found that the fibre diameter of processed fibre ranged from 21.05 mm to 32.82 mm. This indicates that poonchi sheep have a higher diameter than other breeds found in Jammu and Kashmir. There is a dire need for the wool industry in Rajouri and Poonch Districts of Jammu Province. The region needs to be looked after by the Central Sheep and Wool Research Institute. The hilly areas of Latti, Dudu, and Basantgarh in Udhampur district; Billawar, Basohli, and Bani in Kathua district; and most of the areas of Doda and Kishtwar districts are also generating best-quality wool. The quality and quantity of wool per sheep in this region of Jammu Province are commendable as compared with other states of the country.
